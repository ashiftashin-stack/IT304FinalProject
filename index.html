AI Search Engines & Assistants

Exploring Technology, Applications, Ethics, and Future Recommendations

Introduction Applications Ethics Recommendations

Introduction

AI-powered search engines and assistants such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Microsoft Copilot use advanced language models to generate human-like responses. Unlike traditional search engines, these tools interpret and summarize information instead of simply listing links.

This technology matters because it is transforming how people access knowledge, conduct research, and perform work tasks across multiple industries.

Applications

Education

Students use AI tools for tutoring, summarizing content, and generating ideas. While helpful, this raises concerns about academic integrity and over-reliance.

Business & Productivity

Companies use AI assistants to automate writing, coding, and data analysis. Challenges include data privacy and potential job displacement.

Information Search

Users rely on AI for direct answers instead of browsing sources, which can lead to misinformation and reduced critical thinking.

Ethical Discussion

Utilitarianism

AI is beneficial if it maximizes overall good, such as improving efficiency and accessibility. However, misinformation and job loss may reduce its overall benefit.

Deontology

This perspective focuses on rules and responsibilities. Misusing AI for cheating or spreading false information is unethical regardless of outcomes.

Recommendations for Future Use

Technological Improvements

  • Add citations and sources to AI responses
  • Improve bias detection and fairness
  • Develop safeguards against misinformation

Social Strategies

  • Teach AI literacy in schools
  • Encourage critical thinking
  • Promote ethical AI usage guidelines

Policy Recommendations

  • Require AI-generated content disclosure
  • Regulate high-risk applications
  • Establish accountability for misuse
