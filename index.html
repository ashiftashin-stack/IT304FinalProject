AI Search Engines & Assistants

Exploring Technology, Applications, Ethics, and Future Recommendations

By: Ashif Hossain | Kevin Melgar | Josh Greenfield | Ahmad Albakr | Ummay Hani Sultana

Introduction Applications Ethics Recommendations

Introduction

AI-powered search engines and assistants such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Microsoft Copilot use advanced language models to generate human-like responses instead of simply listing search results.

This technology is transforming how people access information, learn, and work across many industries.

Applications

Education

AI tools help students summarize content, generate ideas, and understand complex topics, but raise concerns about academic integrity.

Business & Productivity

Companies use AI for writing, coding, and data analysis, improving efficiency but raising concerns about job displacement and privacy.

Information Search

Users increasingly rely on AI-generated answers instead of traditional search engines, which raises concerns about accuracy and bias.

Ethical Discussion

Utilitarianism

AI is beneficial if it increases overall well-being through efficiency and access to information, but harmful if misinformation spreads widely.

Deontology

Focuses on rules and duties, such as honesty and transparency. Misuse of AI for cheating or deception is unethical regardless of outcomes.

Recommendations for Future Use

Technological Improvements

  • Add citations and verified sources to AI responses
  • Improve bias detection and fairness
  • Reduce hallucinations and misinformation

Social Strategies

  • Teach AI literacy in schools
  • Encourage critical thinking and verification
  • Promote responsible AI usage

Policy Recommendations

  • Require disclosure of AI-generated content
  • Regulate high-risk AI applications
  • Establish accountability for misuse
